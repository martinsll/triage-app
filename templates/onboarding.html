Bienvenido al Entrenamiento de Triaje

En este ejercicio triará grupos de pacientes simulados. Tomará tres tipos de decisiones para cada grupo de cinco pacientes.

1
Orden de selección
Ordena los cinco pacientes de mayor a menor urgencia.
2
Procesos adicionales
Para cada paciente, identifica los procesos adicionales necesarios — como llamar a una respuesta rápida o solicitar una camilla.
3
Destinos
Asigna cada paciente al destino hospitalario correcto — por ejemplo Sala de Riesgo, Sala Vigilada o Quirófano.

Cómo funciona el juego

Cada tarjeta de paciente muestra un conjunto de valores clínicos: frecuencia cardíaca, presión arterial, saturación de oxígeno, frecuencia respiratoria, temperatura, nivel de conciencia, inicio de síntomas, movilidad y contexto social.

Usará estos valores junto con las reglas de triaje (explicadas en la página siguiente) para tomar sus decisiones. Tras enviar sus respuestas, el juego indicará qué fue correcto y qué no.

Puede reintentar cada fase tantas veces como sea necesario. Debe completar las tres fases de cada grupo antes de continuar.

El botón Reglas siempre está visible durante el juego. Puede abrirlo en cualquier momento para consultar las reglas de triaje — úselo tanto como necesite.

Welcome to the Triage Training

In this exercise you will triage groups of simulated patients. You will make three types of decisions for each group of five patients.

1
Selection order
Rank the five patients in the order they should be seen, from most to least urgent.
2
Additional processes
For each patient, identify which additional processes are required — such as calling a rapid response or requesting a stretcher.
3
Destinations
Assign each patient to the correct hospital destination — for example Risk Ward, Monitored Ward, or Surgical Bay.

How the game works

Each patient card shows a set of clinical values: heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate, temperature, alertness, onset, mobility, and social context.

You will use these values together with the triage rules (explained on the next page) to make your decisions. After you submit your answers, the game will tell you what was correct and what was not.

You can retry each phase as many times as needed. You must complete all three phases for each group before moving on.

A Rules button is always visible during the game. You can open it at any time to consult the triage rules — use it as much as you need to.

2. Derivación del riesgo

Cada paciente tiene un nivel de riesgo: Crítico, Moderado o Estable. Depende de la condición del paciente y sus constantes vitales. Estable significa que no se cumplen los criterios de Crítico ni Moderado.

Algunos pacientes tendrán constantes anormales no relevantes para su condición — por ejemplo, fiebre en un paciente Cardiaco. Aplique solo las reglas de su condición específica.

Triage Rules

These are the rules you will apply during the game. Read them carefully — you can consult them at any time during play using the Rules button.

1. Vital sign thresholds

Each vital sign falls into one of three levels. The level determines whether it contributes to a patient's risk. Note that not all vitals are relevant for every condition — only apply the rules for the patient's specific condition.

VitalNormalAbnormalCritical
HR (bpm)60–100101–130>130 or <40
BP (mmHg)90–140>140 or <90—
SpO2 (%)≥9181–90<80
RR (/min)12–2021–30>30 or <10
Temp (°C)36–3838–39>39 or <35

Alertness has three levels: Oriented (normal), Confused (abnormal), Lethargic (critical).

2. Risk derivation

Each patient has a risk level: Critical, Moderate, or Stable. The risk depends on the patient's condition and vital signs. Stable means none of the Critical or Moderate criteria are met.

Some patients will have abnormal vitals that are not relevant for their condition — for example, a high temperature in a Cardiac patient. Apply only the rules for the patient's specific condition and ignore the rest.

3. Selection order

Rank the five patients from most to least urgent using this three-level hierarchy. Only move to the next level when there is a tie at the current level.

PriorityLevel1st2nd3rd
1Risk levelCriticalModerateStable
2Onset (tiebreak)SuddenProgressiveRecurring
3Alertness (tiebreak)LethargicConfusedOriented
AND / OR example: Two patients are both Critical with Sudden onset. To break the tie, look at Alertness — the Lethargic patient goes first, the Oriented patient goes second. All three levels must be checked in order before concluding a tie.
